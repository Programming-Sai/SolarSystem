Facts About The Solar System

  1. The solar system consists of the Sun and all the objects that orbit it.



  2. The Sun is a star, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.


  3. The eight major planets in the solar system, in order from the Sun, are: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.


  4. Pluto was once considered the ninth planet but was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006.



  5. The largest planet is Jupiter, and the smallest planet is Mercury.


  6. Earth is the only planet known to support life.


  7. The Kuiper Belt is a region beyond Neptune containing icy bodies and dwarf planets



  8. The asteroid belt, located between Mars and Jupiter, contains numerous small rocky bodies.


  9. The Oort Cloud is a hypothetical region far beyond the Kuiper Belt, believed to be the source of comets.


  10. The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite.


  11. Saturn's rings are made up of ice particles and rocky debris.


  12. Venus has a thick atmosphere that traps heat, making it the hottest planet in the solar system.


  13. Mars has polar ice caps and a thin atmosphere primarily composed of carbon dioxide.


  14. Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a massive storm that has been raging for centuries.



  15. Uranus and Neptune are referred to as "ice giants" due to their larger proportions of volatile compounds.


  16. The solar wind is a stream of charged particles emitted by the Sun, which creates the heliosphere.



  17. Halley's Comet is one of the most famous periodic comets, visible from Earth approximately every 76 years.



  18. The distance from the Sun to Earth is defined as one astronomical unit (AU).


  19. The solar system is believed to have formed about 4.6 billion years ago from a rotating disk of gas and dust.


  20. Spacecraft like Voyager 1 and 2 have ventured beyond our solar system into interstellar space.
